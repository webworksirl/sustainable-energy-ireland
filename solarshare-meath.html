Solar Panels Ireland Cost

The cost of installing solar panels in Ireland typically falls within the range of €6,000 to €17,000, and this is before applying any available SEAI grants. The exact cost of joining the solar movement in Ireland is influenced by various factors, with two primary ones being the number of photovoltaic panels you choose for your roof and whether you opt for a battery as part of your solar electric system.

To determine the most suitable configuration for your home, you can request a free remote SolarSurvey or provide your details through the form on this page. Solar Rebates This survey assesses your roof's potential for electricity generation, helping you make an informed decision about the number of panels to install.

Moreover, you can save up to €2,400 with an SEAI grant. Eligible homeowners can take advantage of this grant to offset some of the upfront costs. Checking your eligibility is a straightforward process.

A significant development is the introduction of the Clean Export Guarantee scheme. It allows you to be compensated for any excess electricity your panels generate and don't use, which can help recoup installation expenses.

In addition, the government's decision to exempt VAT on solar panel purchase and installation means potential savings of around €1,000 on a €9,000 expenditure.

    Solar Energy = Energy Security

    Solar PV Panels are a reliable source of energy that complements the Irish grid. Installing solar panels on your home doesn't disconnect you from the grid; instead, it reduces your reliance on it. Solar panels empower you to generate your own clean, renewable energy, ensuring a consistent source of power, regardless of external factors.

    Furthermore, solar energy leads to more predictable energy costs. While market fluctuations affect oil and gas prices, solar energy remains free. As energy prices increase over the years due to inflation, your solar panels become an even more valuable investment.

    Battery for Electricity Storage

    In addition to solar panels, you may choose to install a battery. This is crucial if you aim to utilize all the electricity generated in your home. Solar power should either be used immediately or stored, as excess energy is exported to the grid. The battery provides storage for surplus energy until you need it.

    You also have the option to sell any excess energy back to the grid, with different providers offering varying rates per kWh, known as the Feed-In Tariff (FIT). Some homeowners forego batteries to reduce costs and opt to sell excess power back to the grid. An in-depth review of your solar survey data will help determine whether you need a battery.

    3 Ways Solar Panels Benefit Everyone

    By adopting solar energy, you contribute to more than just personal savings. Homeowners who install solar panels provide substantial benefits to society as a whole:

    Economic Boost: By generating their own power, homeowners reduce the need to import fossil fuels, bolstering the local economy and decreasing reliance on foreign energy sources.

    Energy Security: Lower dependence on foreign fuels ensures consistent access to affordable energy, reducing the risk of supply disruptions due to international conflicts or environmental catastrophes abroad.

    Reduced Carbon Footprint: Switching to solar power results in an annual reduction of approximately 1 metric ton of carbon emissions per average home. Over the system's lifetime, this equates to preventing over 25 metric tons of greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to a cleaner environment for future generations.
