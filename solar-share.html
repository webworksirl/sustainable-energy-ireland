Solar Panels Ireland Cost

The cost of installing solar panels in Ireland typically falls within the range of €6,000 to €17,000, and this is before applying any available SEAI grants. The exact cost of joining the solar movement in Ireland is influenced by various factors, with two primary ones being the number of photovoltaic panels you choose for your roof and whether you opt for a battery as part of your solar electric system.

To determine the most suitable configuration for your home, you can request a free remote SolarSurvey or provide your details through the form on this page. This survey assesses your roof's potential for electricity generation, helping you make an informed decision about the number of panels to install.

Moreover, you can save up to €2,400 with an SEAI grant. Eligible homeowners can take advantage of this grant to offset some of the upfront costs. Photovoltaic Cell Checking your eligibility is a straightforward process.

A significant development is the introduction of the Clean Export Guarantee scheme. It allows you to be compensated for any excess electricity your panels generate and don't use, which can help recoup installation expenses.

In addition, the government's decision to exempt VAT on solar panel purchase and installation means potential savings of around €1,000 on a €9,000 expenditure.

Will I get paid for electricity generated and sent to the grid?

Yes, you will be compensated for excess power generated by your panels. In February 2022, legislation was passed in The Dáil to enable the payment of a Feed-In Tariff for surplus electricity sent back to the grid. By the end of August 2022, most companies will declare their rates for this excess power. Some announced rates include Pinergy at 13.5 cents per kWh, SSE Airtricity and Electric Ireland at 14 cents per kWh, Energia at 18 cents per kWh, and Bord Gais at 18.5 cents per kWh.
