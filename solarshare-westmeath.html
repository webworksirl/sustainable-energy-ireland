How much electricity can you generate on your roof?

The chart below, sourced from the Solis app used by some of our customers, records the solar power generated on a 5.4kW system located on the Monaghan-Fermanagh border in March 2022. It shows a remarkable 672.8 kWh generated during this period.

The average Irish home can generate up to 80% of its electricity requirements through solar PV panels. Considering the average annual electricity bill of almost €1,100, this equates to potential savings of €880 annually.

Will I get paid for electricity generated and sent to the grid?

Yes, you will be compensated for excess power generated by your panels. In February 2022, legislation was passed in The Dáil to enable the payment of a Feed-In Tariff for surplus electricity sent back to the grid. By the end of August 2022, most companies will declare their rates for this excess power. Some announced rates include Pinergy at 13.5 cents per kWh, SSE Airtricity and Electric Ireland at 14 cents per kWh, Energia at 18 cents per kWh, and Bord Gais at 18.5 cents per kWh. Flogas offers the highest rate at 20 cents per kWh. This is another compelling reason why solar panels make sense in Ireland.

However, it's advisable to use as much of the power you generate in your own home, as the compensation for selling electricity back to the grid is lower than the cost of buying electricity. Batteries provide an effective solution for storing excess solar energy for future use.
