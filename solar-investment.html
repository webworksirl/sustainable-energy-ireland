    Will I get paid for electricity generated and sent to the grid?

    Yes, you will be compensated for excess power generated by your panels. In February 2022, legislation was passed in The Dáil to enable the payment of a Feed-In Tariff for surplus electricity sent back to the grid. By the end of August 2022, most companies will declare their rates for this excess power. Solar Installation Costs Some announced rates include Pinergy at 13.5 cents per kWh, SSE Airtricity and Electric Ireland at 14 cents per kWh, Energia at 18 cents per kWh, and Bord Gais at 18.5 cents per kWh. Flogas offers the highest rate at 20 cents per kWh. This is another compelling reason why solar panels make sense in Ireland.

    However, it's advisable to use as much of the power you generate in your own home, as the compensation for selling electricity back to the grid is lower than the cost of buying electricity. Batteries provide an effective solution for storing excess solar energy for future use.

    Battery for Electricity Storage

    In addition to solar panels, you may choose to install a battery. This is crucial if you aim to utilize all the electricity generated in your home. Solar power should either be used immediately or stored, as excess energy is exported to the grid. The battery provides storage for surplus energy until you need it.

    You also have the option to sell any excess energy back to the grid, with different providers offering varying rates per kWh, known as the Feed-In Tariff (FIT). Some homeowners forego batteries to reduce costs and opt to sell excess power back to the grid. An in-depth review of your solar survey data will help determine whether you need a battery.
